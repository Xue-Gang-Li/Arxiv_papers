摘要：二级系统缺陷（TLSs）在介电材料中被认为是超导电路去相干的主要来源。然而，这些缺陷的原子形态、频率分布和偶极矩等详细信息仍然较少了解。一个重要原因是，目前探测TLS缺陷需要使用谐振器或量子比特，这些探测器需要复杂的多阶段制造工艺，并且需要在稀释冰箱中冷却后进行测量以推断其相干时间。此外，即使是可调频率的探测器也只能获取TLS缺陷在其模式体积和狭窄带宽内的信息。因此，关于TLS缺陷在隔离材料和界面中的原子起源的详细了解仍然缺乏，这使得从第一性原则设计下一代超导设备和介电材料变得具有挑战性。本研究中，我们介绍了一种新方法，可以使用宽带3D波导探测TLS缺陷集体在低温下的介电响应。这种技术提供了关于TLS缺陷集体动力学中的记忆效应的证据，这可能有助于提取有关基础TLS缺陷起源及其相互作用的关键信息。重要的是，这种新型分光谱技术可以在制造过程的所有阶段使用，并最终为减少TLS缺陷对超导电路的影响提供缓解策略。因此，这项技术将有助于深入了解TLS缺陷的原子起源，这对于量子信息科学和更广泛的材料科学界具有深远意义。

                            Hardware-tailored logical Clifford circuits for stabilizer codes

                            Eric J. Kuehnke, Kyano Levi, Joschka Roffe, Jens Eisert, Daniel Miller
                            摘要：This paper focuses on quantum error correction, which is essential for protecting fragile quantum information through suitable encoding and active interventions. After encoding k logical qubits into n > k physical qubits using a stabilizer code, the process involves measuring stabilizers, decoding syndromes, and applying appropriate corrections. Although this method can protect quantum information, manipulating encoded quantum data without introducing uncorrectable errors is notoriously difficult. The paper introduces a mathematical framework for constructing hardware-tailored quantum circuits that implement any desired Clifford unitary on the logical level of any given stabilizer code. The main contribution is formulating this task as a discrete optimization problem, which can explicitly integrate arbitrary hardware connectivity constraints. A key feature of this framework is the natural incorporation of optimization over all Clifford gauges (differing only in their action outside the code space) for a desired logical circuit. For example, fault-tolerant and teleportation-free logical Hadamard circuits for the [[8,3,2]] code are developed. This work shifts focus from the standard generator decomposition approach to holistic compilation of entire logical circuits, leading to significant practical savings. The paper also introduces both necessary mathematics and open-source software to compile hardware-tailored logical Clifford circuits for stabilizer codes.
