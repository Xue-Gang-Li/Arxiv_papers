            Hierarchy of entanglement detection criteria for random high-dimensional states

            Akhil Kumar Awasthi, Sudipta Mondal, Rivu Gupta, Aditi Sen De
            摘要：This study focuses on the detection of entanglement in quantum systems, particularly for non-positive partially transposed (NPT) states. The authors establish a hierarchy among various detection criteria, including entropy-based, majorization, realignment, and reduction methods, analyzing their performance based on state rank and subsystem dimensions. They prove lower bounds on the rank of mixed quantum states beyond which certain criteria fail to detect entanglement. The effectiveness of these methods is evaluated using three key indicators: fraction of detected states, mean detectable entanglement, and minimum required entanglement. The results provide insights into entanglement thresholds for reliable detection, showing that beyond a certain level of entanglement, all criteria become equally effective for low-rank states, while a hierarchy among the criteria emerges with moderate to high ranks. Interestingly, the proposed ordering of the considered criteria in qubit-qudit systems differs from that in higher dimensions. Additionally, it is shown that detection efficiency is influenced by subsystem dimension asymmetry, as the realignment criterion behaves more efficiently when subsystem dimension differences are small.
