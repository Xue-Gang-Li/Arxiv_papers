      Dynamical Phase Transitions in Open Quantum Walks

      Stefano Longhi
      摘要：The study of dynamic phase transitions in the relaxation behavior of stochastic quantum walks reveals that when coherent unitary evolution is periodically interrupted by dephasing, the dynamics become classicalized and can be described by non-equilibrium Markovian processes. These processes violate detailed balance and exhibit a richer spectral structure compared to equilibrium systems. Extending recent insights from classical Markov dynamics, this work demonstrates that quantum-classical hybrid systems can host both first-order dynamical phase transitions (characterized by eigenvalue crossings) and second-order transitions marked by the coalescence of eigenvalues and eigenvectors at exceptional points. Two paradigmatic models are analyzed: a quantum walk on a ring under gauge fields and a walk on a finite line with internal degrees of freedom, both showing distinct mechanisms for breaking detailed balance. These findings reveal a novel class of critical behavior in open quantum systems, where decoherence-induced classicalization enables access to non-Hermitian spectral phenomena. The results have promising implications for quantum technologies, including quantum simulation, error mitigation, and the engineering of controllable non-equilibrium quantum states.
