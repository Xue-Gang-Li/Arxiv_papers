                    Coordinating Decisions via Quantum Telepathy

                    Dawei Ding, Liang Jiang
                    摘要：This work presents a conceptual framework for applying quantum telepathy to real-world problems, particularly in scenarios where multiple parties need to coordinate decisions without communication. The authors highlight the example of high-frequency trading (HFT), where trades are made at microsecond timescales, but speed-of-light delays between stock exchanges range from 100 microseconds to 10 milliseconds. They argue that experimental realization of quantum telepathy schemes capable of providing a quantum advantage for real-world problems is almost immediately possible due to the maturity of Bell inequality violation experiments. A case study is conducted for a concrete HFT scenario, which generalizes the CHSH game, and evaluates different physical implementations for achieving a quantum advantage. The work emphasizes that violating the CHSH inequality only requires single-qubit operations on two entangled physical qubits, demonstrating that fault tolerance is not necessary to realize a quantum advantage.
