Nonclassicality of a Macroscopic Qubit-Ensemble via Parity Measurement Induced Disturbance

Lorenzo Braccini, Debarshi Das, Ben Zindorf, Stephen D. Hogan, John J. L. Morton, Sougato Bose
摘要：我们提出了一种实验方案，通过违反经典宏观实在性（MR）的概念来测试宏观量子比特集的非经典性，该方法利用了对量子系统进行测量时引起的基本扰动。使用电磁谐振器来探测量子比特集合的奇偶性。连续测量的过程使得整个集合的非经典性得以表现出来，在理想情况下，不论其大小如何，都能实现这一点。这使我们能够探索量子力学的宏观极限，因为量子比特集实际上是一个拥有许多ħ单位的大自旋体。即使当ħ相对于集合的总角动量趋近于0时，在无噪声情况下仍然会出现MR的一定违反程度。然而，环境退相干和量子比特-电磁场耦合不均匀性加速了从量子到经典的过渡。这意味着波尔的对应原理并非基本，而是实际限制的结果。我们概述了一种多种量子比特（超导量子比特、半导体中的自旋和 Rydberg 原子）与共面波导谐振器耦合的实现方法，并通过相应的噪声分析发现，使用当前技术可以通过违反MR检测到多达100个量子比特的非经典性。
https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.15880

    How "Quantum" is your Quantum Computer? Macrorealism-based Benchmarking via Mid-Circuit Parity Measurements

    Ben Zindorf, Lorenzo Braccini, Debarshi Das, Sougato Bose
    摘要：为了进行有意义的计算，量子计算机（QCs）必须扩展到宏观水平，即达到大量量子比特，这是大多数量子公司追求的目标。如何在这些规模下有效测试它们的量子特性？我们展示了宏观现实主义（MR）的违反提供了一条有希望实现这一目标的途径，即经典系统具有可以在不干扰的情况下测量的明确属性的事实。无干扰条件（NDC）是用于测试MR的一种平等方法，通过对N个量子比特进行两次连续的奇偶性测量，可以发现其在理想条件下的违反与N无关。然而，现实中的有噪声的量子计算机显示出随着N增加而从量子到经典的过渡，为可扩展的基准测试指标提供了根本动机。两种方法被制定用于实现此指标：一种涉及中电路测量，探索波函数的不可逆崩溃，与另一种产生可逆纠缠不同的方法。两种方法都设计为无笨拙漏洞，即不需要考虑经典干扰。MR的违反在IBM QC上被检测到，最高达到了N=38个量子比特，将已知结果提升了一个数量级。使用提出的NDC指标对两台量子计算机进行了基准测试，显示从一代到下一代，其量子特性有三倍的改善。

            Explicit construction of low-overhead gadgets for gates on quantum LDPC codes

            Paul Webster, Samuel C. Smith, Lawrence Z. Cohen
            摘要：Quantum low-density parity check (QLDPC) codes can significantly reduce the overhead of quantum computing, provided the methods for performing logical operations do not require substantial space and time resources.                     Medusa: Detecting and Removing Failures for Scalable Quantum Computing

                    Karoliina Oksanen, Quan Hoang, Alexandru Paler
                    摘要：Quantum circuits will experience failures that lead to computational errors. We introduce Medusa, an automated compilation method for lowering a circuit's failure rate. Medusa uses flags to predict the absence of high-weight errors. Our method can numerically upper bound the failure rate of a circuit in the presence of flags, and fine tune the fault-tolerance of the flags in order to reach this bound. We assume the flags can have an increased fault-tolerance as a result of applying surface QECs to the gates interacting with them. We use circuit level depolarizing noise to evaluate the effectiveness of these flags in revealing the absence of the high-weight stabilizers. Medusa reduces the cost of quantum-error-correction (QEC) because the underlying circuit has a lower failure rate. We benchmark our approach using structured quantum circuits representative of ripple-carry adders. In particular, our flag scheme demonstrates that for adder-like circuits, the failure rate of large-scale implementations can be lowered to fit the failure rates of smaller-scale circuits. We show numerically that a slight improvement in the local fault-tolerance of the flag-qubits can lead to a reduction in the overall failure rate of the entire quantum circuit.
