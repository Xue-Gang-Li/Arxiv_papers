Bell-Inequality Violation for Continuous, Non-Projective Measurements

Shalender Singh, Santosh Kumar
摘要：许多固态量子平台不允许进行清晰的、投影性的测量，而是在弱的非毁灭性读取下产生连续的电压或场迹。对于这样的系统，基于二元投影测量的标准贝尔测试无法直接应用，因此如何从连续时间序列数据中证明量子非局域性成为一个问题。本文开发了一般理论框架，展示了贝尔-CHSH不等式违反可以在没有假设任何特定崩溃模型或相位分布的情况下，从连续、非投影测量中提取。我们表明，对单个纠缠对的足够长时间的持续测量可以采样其内部相位-概率结构，使得通过相位敏感投影和粗化处理构建有效的二元可观测量。所得到的贝尔关联器由两个实验上可访问的资源控制：固有的单量子比特相位扩散和量子比特之间的非局域相位锁定。我们通过使用Qiskit进行量子电路模拟来实现传统投影测量CHSH测试对所得到的估计值进行基准测试，发现它们在贝尔违反区域能够达成量化协议，而无需参数调整。经典决定性相关性无法违反CHSH界限，而量子相位锁定系统恢复了纠缠特征的非线性角度依赖性。我们的研究结果为在测量本质上是连续且弱的情况下展示贝尔非局域性提供了一条实用路径。

          The Lieb-Robinson correlation function for long disordered transverse-field Ising chains

          Brendan J. Mahoney, Craig S. Lent
          摘要：The transverse-field Ising model is useful for studying interacting qubit arrays. The Lieb--Robinson correlation function can be used to characterize the propagation of quantum information in Ising chains. Considerable work has been done to establish bounds on this correlation function in various circumstances. To actually calculate the value of the correlation function directly typically requires a state space which grows exponentially with system size, and so is intractable for all but relatively small systems. We employ a recently-developed method that enables direct calculation of the value of the Lieb--Robinson correlation function and which scales linearly with system size. This enables the computation for systems with many hundreds of qubits, revealing the propagation of quantum information down the chain. We extend this technique to the problem of Ising chains with randomly disordered coupling strengths. Increasing disorder causes localization of the quantum correlations and halts propagation of quantum information

                Modeling Noise in Quantum Computing of Scalar Convection

                Jiahua Yang, Zhen Lu, Yue Yang
                摘要：This study investigates the potential of quantum computing to accelerate fluid dynamics simulations and explores how hardware noise in current quantum systems affects simulation accuracy. The researchers focused on analyzing the specific physical effects of quantum noise on flow simulation results, particularly for one-dimensional scalar convection. They employed a quantum spectral algorithm that isolates noise-induced artifacts in spectral magnitudes by ensuring ideal time advancement only affects Fourier phases. A theoretical transition matrix based on Hamming distances between computational basis states was derived to predict spectral decay, validated through density-matrix simulations and experiments on a superconducting quantum processor. These findings suggest that quantum errors can be modeled as deterministic physical terms rather than purely stochastic perturbations.
