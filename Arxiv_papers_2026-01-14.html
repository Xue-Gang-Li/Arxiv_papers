Fault-Tolerant Quantum Error Correction: Implementing Hamming-Based Codes with Advanced Syndrome Extraction Techniques

Soham Bhadra, Diyansha Singh, Angana Chowdhury
摘要：构建可靠的量子计算机需要保护脆弱的量子态免受不可避免的环境噪声和操作错误的影响。虽然像Steane $[\![7,1,3]\!]$代码这样的量子纠错码提供了优雅的理论解决方案，但它们的实际成功在很大程度上取决于我们如何测量错误——这个过程被称为综合提取。挑战在于用于测量的辅助量子比特（ancilla qubits）：当它们失败时，错误会在整个量子系统中蔓延，破坏我们试图保护的信息。我们通过实施和比较三种复杂的综合测量策略来解决这个根本问题：Shor的猫态方法，将测量分布在多个纠缠辅助量子比特上，准备成功率达到85-92%；Steane的编码辅助量子比特方法，使用完整的错误纠正逻辑量子比特，综合保真度达到97.8%；以及一个灵活的统一框架，该框架根据硬件功能调整策略。通过使用IBM的Qiskit平台进行广泛的模拟，涵盖随机化基准测试和T重电路，我们证明了智能辅助量子比特管理可以将错误抑制提高多达2.4倍，相比标准方法。在现实噪声条件下，物理错误率为$10^{-3}$，我们的实现逻辑错误率低至$5.1 \times 10^{-5}$，同时即使对于深度电路也保持接近1的逻辑保真度（0.99997）。阈值分析显示，从距离-3到距离-13的代码具有强大的性能，其特征阈值曲线显示在临界物理错误率以下呈现指数级的错误抑制。这些结果为短期内的量子设备提供了可以立即部署的工具，并为构建容错量子计算机的扩展奠定了实际设计原则。

                Entanglement-swapping measurements for deterministic entanglement distribution

                Mir Alimuddin, Jaemin Kim, Acín, Leonardo Zambrano
                摘要：Entanglement swapping is a key primitive for distributing entanglement across nodes in quantum networks. In standard protocols, the outcome of the intermediate measurement determines the resulting state, making the process inherently probabilistic and requiring postselection. In this work, we fully characterize those measurements under which entanglement swapping becomes deterministic: for arbitrary pure inputs, every measurement outcome produces local-unitarily equivalent states. We also show that an optimal measurement, maximizing a concurrence-type entanglement measure, is built from complex Hadamard matrices. For this optimal protocol, we provide a complete, dimension-dependent classification of deterministic entanglement-swapping measurements: unique in dimensions $d=2,3$, infinite for $d=4$, and comprising $72$ inequivalent classes for $d=5$. We further consider a general network with multiple swapping nodes and show that, for $d=2,3$ the resulting end-to-end state is independent of the order in which the repeaters perform the optimal measurements. Our results establish optimal entanglement-swapping schemes that are post-selection free, in the sense that they distribute entanglement across generic quantum network architectures without unfavorable measurement outcomes.
