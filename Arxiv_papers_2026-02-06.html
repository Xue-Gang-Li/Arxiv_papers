Scalable testing of quantum error correction

John Zhuoyang Ye, Jens Palsberg
摘要：The standard method for benchmarking quantum error-correction is randomized fault-injection testing. The state-of-the-art tool \stim is efficient for error correction implementations with distances of up to 10, but scales poorly to larger distances for low physical error rates. In this paper, we present a scalable approach that combines stratified fault injection with extrapolation. Our insight is that some of the fault space can be sampled efficiently, after which extrapolation is sufficient to complete the testing task. As a result, our tool scales to distance 17 for a physical error rate of 0.0005 with a two-hour time budget on a desktop. For this case, it estimated a logical error rate of $1.51 \times 10^{-11}$ with high confidence.

      Distributed Quantum Error Mitigation: Global and Local ZNE encodings

      Maria Gragera Garces
      摘要：错误是阻碍实际量子计算的主要瓶颈。在分布式量子计算中，量子网络引入的额外通信噪声加剧了这一挑战。虽然零噪声外推 (ZNE) 等错误缓解技术在单独的量子处理器上表现有效，但其在分布式架构中的行为尚不清楚。我们通过比较全局优化（电路划分前应用 ZNE）和本地优化（对每个子电路独立应用 ZNE），研究了分布式环境中的 ZNE。划分是在单一电路上完成的，然后通过插入基于噪声 teleportation 的通信原语将其转换为分布式实现。我们评估了两种方法在不同数量的量子处理单元 (QPUs) 和异构本地和网络噪声条件下的性能。结果表明，Global ZNE 具有更好的扩展性，在六个 QPUs 上实现了最高 48% 的错误减少。此外，我们观察到反直觉的噪声行为，即增加 QPU 的数量可以提高缓解效果，尽管通信开销更高。这些发现强调了分布式量子错误缓解中的基本权衡，并就电路结构、划分策略和网络噪声之间的相互作用提出新的问题。

        Gradient Analysis of Barren Plateau in Parameterized Quantum Circuits with multi-qubit gates

        Yuhan Yao, Yoshihiko Hasegawa
        摘要：The emergence of the Barren Plateau phenomenon poses a significant challenge to quantum machine learning. While most Barren Plateau analyses focus on single-qubit rotation gates, the gradient behavior of Parameterized Quantum Circuits built from multi-qubit gates remains largely unexplored. In this work, we present a general theoretical framework for analyzing the gradient properties of Parameterized Quantum Circuits with multi-qubit gates. Our method generalizes the direct computation framework, bypassing the Haar random assumption on parameters and enabling the calculation of the gradient expectation and variance. We apply this framework to single-layer and deep-layer circuits, deriving analytical results that quantify how gradient variance is co-determined by the size of the multi-qubit gate and the number of qubits, layers, and effective parameters. Numerical simulations validate our findings. Our study provides a refined framework for analyzing and optimizing Parameterized Quantum Circuits with complex multi-qubit gates.
